# IEC 60599:2022 — interpretation of dissolved & free gases (cite-only)

- **Citation:** *Mineral oil-filled electrical equipment in service — Guidance on the interpretation
  of dissolved and free gases analysis*, IEC 60599:2022 (Ed. 4.0). `bib: iec60599`.
- **Type:** **standard, cite-only** — paid, **PDF not acquired** (deliberately, see `docs/refs/README.md`).

## TL;DR
The source of the **ratio method** thresholds we implement. We cite it but do not hold the PDF; its
limits are reproduced in the papers we do hold, so this is not a blocker.

## What it contains (and where we already have it)
- The **IEC three-ratio method**: R1 = C2H2/C2H4, R2 = CH4/H2, R5 = C2H4/C2H6, with a threshold table
  mapping ratio ranges to the fault codes (PD, D1, D2, T1, T2, T3).
- The **fault-code definitions** themselves (PD/D1/D2/T1/T2/T3), also stated in `duval2002review`.
- Typical-value and "90 % percentile" gas concentration ranges per equipment type.

## How we use it without the PDF
- The IEC ratio logic is implemented in `src/dga/conventional.py` (one of our baselines/labels).
- Exact threshold values are reproduced in **Saleh et al. 2026 (Tables 1–3)** and the **Duval**
  articles (held in `docs/refs/`) — verify any directly-quoted limit against one of those before it
  enters the paper.

## Relevance to our project
- A **baseline + label source** (IEC ratios), and a **citation** for the fault taxonomy. No primary
  content gap for our needs.
