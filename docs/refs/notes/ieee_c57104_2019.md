# IEEE C57.104-2019 — Guide for the Interpretation of Gases (the DGA standard)

- **Citation:** *IEEE Guide for the Interpretation of Gases Generated in Mineral Oil-Immersed
  Transformers*, IEEE Std C57.104-2019. `bib: ieee_c57104` · PDF `docs/refs/ieee_c57104_2019.pdf`.
- **Type:** **standard** (long; skim for the key tables, do not read cover-to-cover).

## TL;DR
The modern statistical reframing of "is this transformer's gassing normal?", based on a population
of >1 M lab DGA results. Its **percentile limits** and **generation-rate** logic are the
domain-standard analogue of our data-driven **severity + anomaly** scoring.

## What it contains (the parts we use)
- **3 DGA Status levels** (1 = probably normal, 2 = suspicious, 3 = serious) — simplified from the 4
  "conditions" of the 2008/1991 editions.
- **Table 1 = 90th-percentile** gas levels; **Table 2 = 95th-percentile** levels;
  **Table 3 = 95th-percentile of deltas** between consecutive samples;
  **Table 4 = 95th-percentile of multi-point generation rates (ppm/year)**.
- Norms are **stratified by transformer age and O2/N2 ratio** (sealed vs free-breathing).
- The 2019 edition **embeds the Duval Triangle & Pentagon** (Annex D), **removes
  Doernenburg/key-gas** from the main text, **drops TDCG/TCG** interpretation, and adds
  **Normalized Energy Intensity (NEI)** (Annex F).

## Limitations (for us)
- **Cite-only for exact thresholds** unless re-verified; numbers are population-statistical and
  condition-dependent.

## Relevance to our project
- **Framing for the severity / anomaly step:** a reconstruction-error or percentile threshold is our
  **data-driven analogue** of the standard's 90th/95th-percentile "status" levels.
- **Generation rates (Table 4)** justify our **temporal feature** = per-unit ppm/year gas-rate
  (`src/dga/temporal.py`).
- **Caveat to acknowledge in the paper:** the standard's "normal" depends on **age and O2/N2**, which
  we currently exclude — worth a sentence that "normal is condition-dependent."
